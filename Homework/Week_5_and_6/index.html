Mark van den Hoven 10533133, bron: stats.oecd.org

This graph shows the connection between the well-being and life expectancy of a Country with the following factors: Dwellings without basic faculties, employment rate, educational attainment, years in education and air pollution. It seems that the lower the amount of dwellings without basic faculties, the higher the well-being and the life-expectancy. Also a higher employment rate and higher educational attainment seems to be a factor for higher well-being and life expectancy. Years in education does not seem to be an important factor. A higher amount of air pollution seems to give lower well-being and life expectancy.
